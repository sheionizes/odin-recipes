Oatmeal Pecan Coconut Cookies

Description

These cookies are my husband's favorites, especially when they have a good crunch. The cookies are great for those in your life that don't enjoy chocolate and just everyone. They aren't too sweet, which seems to be a theme in my favorite desert recipes. The recipe is an adaption from an original Betty Crocker 70's oatmeal cookie recipe. Enjoy!

  • 1/2 Cup nuts walnut or pecans (chopped)
  • /2 Cup unsweetened coconut (shredded)
  • 1/3 Cup Brown sugar
  • 1/3 Cup white sugar
  • 1 Cup quick oats
  • 1 Cup old fashioned oats
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (add additional 1/3 tsp when using unsalted butter)
  • 1 stick melted butter (1/2C) (salted) / 113 grams
  • 2 eggs
  1. Preheat oven: 350 degrees/176.67 Cel.
  2. Mix together all dry ingredients.
  3. Add wet ingredients next - I've found that this really has an impact on the way the cookies turn out.
  4. Use spoon to drop balls on a parchment paper lined cookie sheet - the dough will be sticky but doesn't stick together that well. I usually flatten mine a little bit so they aren't so rounded. I like them smaller and bigger, you just need to see how large you'd like them - they don't spread out much during cooking.
  5. Bake for ~20 minutes, until the bottom and top of cookies are a deep golden brown - but not burnt.
  6. Remove to cool on a cookie sheet.
